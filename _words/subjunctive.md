definition: |
  Subjunctive is a grammatical mood expressing hypothesis, wish, doubt, or condition contrary to fact. From Latin subjunctivus, subjoined. In English, it survives in set phrases: "If I were you," "God save the King," "Be that as it may." More prominent in Romance languages. Used in grammar, linguistics, and language teaching. Formal and technical&nbsp;register.
