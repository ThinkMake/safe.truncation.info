definition: |
  Woodblock is a block of wood used as a surface for printing, or a percussion instrument consisting of a hollow wooden block struck with a mallet. In printmaking, a woodblock is carved with a design and inked for relief printing. The technique is ancient, originating in East Asia. In music, woodblocks are used in orchestral and percussive&nbsp;settings.
