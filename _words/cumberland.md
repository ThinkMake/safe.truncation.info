definition: |
  Cumberland is a historic county in northwest England, now part of the administrative county of Cumbria. Known for the Lake District, Hadrian's Wall, and Cumberland sausage, a coiled pork sausage with protected geographical indication. The name derives from Cymru, the Welsh name for Wales, reflecting ancient Brythonic settlement. Also a common place name in North&nbsp;America.
