definition: |
  Analogy is a comparison between two things to explain or clarify a concept, highlighting shared features despite difference in kind. From Greek analogia, meaning proportion. Used in rhetoric, teaching, science, and philosophy. Analogies simplify the unfamiliar by relating it to the known. Register spans formal argument to everyday&nbsp;explanation.
