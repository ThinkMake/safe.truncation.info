definition: |
  Cumin is a flowering plant whose dried seeds are widely used as a spice, especially in South Asian, Middle Eastern, and Latin American cuisines. From Latin cuminum, from Greek kyminon. The seeds have a warm, earthy, slightly bitter flavour. Available whole or ground. One of the world's most consumed spices, foundational to curry powder and chilli&nbsp;blends.
