Woodpecker is a bird of the family Picidae, known for its habit of pecking at tree bark to find insects and to excavate nesting cavities. Characterised by a strong bill, stiff tail feathers for support, and a rapid drumming sound. Around 240 species exist worldwide. The green woodpecker, great spotted woodpecker, and lesser spotted woodpecker are native to&nbsp;Britain.
