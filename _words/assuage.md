Assuage means to make an unpleasant feeling less intense, or to satisfy an appetite. From Old French assoagier, from Latin suavis, sweet. Used in formal and literary writing to describe easing grief, guilt, or hunger. One assuages fears, assuages anger, or assuages thirst. Formal register; rarely used in casual&nbsp;speech.
