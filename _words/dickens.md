definition: |
  Dickens refers primarily to Charles Dickens (1812–1870), the English novelist whose works include Oliver Twist, A Tale of Two Cities, and Great Expectations. His name is also used in mild exclamations: "what the dickens" is a euphemism for "what the devil," predating the novelist. Dickensian describes conditions or characters resembling those in his novels, often involving poverty or grotesque&nbsp;bureaucracy.
