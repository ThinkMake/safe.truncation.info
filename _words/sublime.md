Sublime describes something of such excellence or grandeur as to inspire awe, or in aesthetics, the quality of overwhelming greatness. From Latin sublimis, high, lofty. A key concept in Romantic philosophy and art criticism, theorised by Burke and Kant. Also used informally as a superlative: "the food was sublime." Spans philosophical technical use to casual high&nbsp;praise.
