definition: |
  Polemic is a strong verbal or written attack on someone's opinions, or the practice of engaging in such controversy. From Greek polemikos, warlike, from polemos, war. As an adjective, polemical. Used in theology, politics, and literary criticism. A polemic makes an aggressive case rather than a balanced argument. Formal register; associated with forceful intellectual&nbsp;debate.
