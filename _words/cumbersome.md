definition: |
  Cumbersome means large or heavy and difficult to carry, use, or manage. From cumber, to obstruct or burden, plus -some. Used to describe bulky objects, unwieldy systems, or overly complicated processes. "The cumbersome bureaucracy" and "cumbersome equipment" are typical uses. Carries a mildly negative register. Common in formal and journalistic&nbsp;writing.
