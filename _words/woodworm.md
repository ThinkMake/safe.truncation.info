Woodworm is the larva of a wood-boring beetle, particularly the common furniture beetle (Anobium punctatum), which tunnels through and damages wooden structures and furniture. The term also refers to the damage caused by such larvae, visible as small round holes in the wood surface. Treatment involves insecticide application. A common problem in older buildings in&nbsp;Britain.
