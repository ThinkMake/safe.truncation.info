definition: |
  Cumulus is a type of cloud characterised by heaped, rounded masses with flat bases, typically white and associated with fair weather at low altitude. From Latin cumulus, a heap. Defined formally in meteorology; the towering cumulonimbus variety produces thunderstorms. Cumulus clouds are among the most recognisable and commonly depicted cloud forms. Plural:&nbsp;cumuli.
