  Babel refers to a confused noise made by many voices, or a confused mixture of languages. Derived from the biblical Tower of Babel, in which God scattered humanity by multiplying languages. From Hebrew Bavel, meaning Babylon. Used figuratively in English for linguistic or communicative chaos. Also a proper noun for the ancient city and the biblical&nbsp;story.
