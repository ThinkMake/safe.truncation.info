definition: |
  Buttresses are structures built against a wall to support or reinforce it, particularly in Gothic architecture. From Old French bouter, to thrust. A flying buttress transfers thrust from a high wall across an arch to an outer pier. As a verb, to buttress means to support or strengthen an argument or structure. Used in architecture and figurative&nbsp;speech.
