Arsenic is a chemical element, symbol As, atomic number 33, classified as a metalloid. From Greek arsenikon, via Arabic. Historically used as a poison and in medicine; now found in pesticides, semiconductors, and wood preservatives. Arsenic compounds occur naturally in soil and water. Toxic in most forms; closely associated with poisoning in historical&nbsp;literature.
