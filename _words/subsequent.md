Subsequent means coming after something in time or order. From Latin subsequi, to follow. Used in formal and professional writing: "subsequent events," "in subsequent years." More formal than "later" or "next." The adverb subsequently is widely used in academic and legal writing to describe what follows. Neutral formal register; rarely used in casual&nbsp;speech.
