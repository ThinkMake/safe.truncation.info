Subtract means to take a number or amount away from another. From Latin subtrahere, to draw from beneath. A basic arithmetic operation, the inverse of addition. Used in mathematics, accounting, and figurative speech. "Subtract the cost from the total," "subtract years from one's appearance." The noun is subtraction. Neutral register; most common in mathematical and educational&nbsp;contexts.
