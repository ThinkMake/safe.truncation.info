Subprime describes financial products or borrowers who do not meet standard credit criteria, carrying higher risk and higher interest rates. From sub- plus prime. The subprime mortgage market in the US expanded dramatically in the early 2000s, contributing to the 2007–08 financial crisis. "Subprime lending" and "subprime borrower" are the common collocations.
