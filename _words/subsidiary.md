definition: |
  Subsidiary is a company controlled by a parent company, or something that supplements or supports a more important thing. From Latin subsidiarius, of reserves. In business, a subsidiary is a separate legal entity majority-owned by a parent. As an adjective, subsidiary means secondary or supplementary. Used in corporate law, finance, and general professional contexts. Neutral formal&nbsp;register.
