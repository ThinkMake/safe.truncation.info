Subdue means to overcome, quieten, or bring under control. From Latin subducere, to draw away, influenced by Latin subdere, to subject. Used for overpowering a person, quelling an emotion, or reducing intensity. Subdued light is dimmed; a subdued voice is quiet; a subdued person is meek. Carries implications of force or suppression. Neutral to formal&nbsp;register.
