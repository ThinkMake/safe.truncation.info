Dominican refers to two things: a member of the Dominican Order of friars founded by Saint Dominic in the 13th century, or a person from the Dominican Republic or Dominica. The Dominican Republic occupies the eastern half of Hispaniola; Dominica is a separate island nation. Context distinguishes the religious from the national&nbsp;sense.
