definition: |
  Tithe is a tenth part of income or produce, historically paid as a tax to support the church. From Old English teogotha, tenth. Once compulsory in England, tithes were a major source of church revenue and a source of social tension until their commutation in the 19th century. Today, tithing continues as a voluntary practice in many Christian&nbsp;traditions.
